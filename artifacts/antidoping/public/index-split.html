The Testing Process

Transparent, rigorous, and respectful. Know what to expect during a doping control session.

1. Notification

A Doping Control Officer (DCO) can notify you for a test anytime, anywhere. Show your ID and stay in their sight until the process is complete.

Elite athletes in a Registered Testing Pool must provide a daily 60-minute time slot for whereabouts testing.

2. Sample Collection

You choose a sealed collection kit. The sample is split into an 'A' and 'B' bottle — both sealed in front of you.

Urine requires 90mL minimum. Blood is collected by a qualified Blood Collection Officer (BCO).

3. Analysis & Results

Declare all medications taken in the last 7 days on the Doping Control Form, then sign to confirm the procedure.

Samples are analyzed anonymously at a WADA-accredited lab. If an Adverse Analytical Finding is returned, you will be notified and can request 'B' sample analysis.

Your Rights During Testing

  • • Have a representative or interpreter present
  • • Ask for more information about the process
  • • Request modifications if you are a minor or have an impairment
  • • Document any concerns on the Doping Control Form

FAQs

What is strict liability?

Athletes are responsible for any prohibited substance in their body, regardless of intent.

Can I refuse a test?

Refusing without compelling justification is an Anti-Doping Rule Violation (ADRV).

Are supplements safe?

No supplement is 100% safe — they carry a high risk of contamination.

How long is a TUE valid?

Usually 1–4 years, depending on the medical condition.
